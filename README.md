Based on the data preparation and Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of the Telco dataset, here are the top 5 business insights:

The Contract Trap: Customers on Month-to-Month contracts have a drastically higher churn rate (over 40%) compared to those locked into One-Year or Two-Year contracts. Encouraging users to switch to annual plans should be the primary retention strategy.

Pricing Sensitivity: The Boxplot reveals that churned customers generally have higher Monthly Charges than retained customers. Customers paying a premium are more likely to shop around for cheaper competitors.

The "New Customer" Risk: Churn is heavily concentrated in the 0–12 Months Tenure Group. If a customer survives their first year, their likelihood of churning drops exponentially. Onboarding and first-year loyalty programs are critical.

Internet Service Type Matters: Fiber Optic internet customers churn at a notably higher rate than DSL customers. This suggests potential underlying issues with Fiber Optic pricing, reliability, or customer expectations versus reality.

Lack of Add-On Services Increases Risk: Customers who do not have additional services like Online Security, Tech Support, or Device Protection are highly correlated with churn. Bundling these services into basic packages could create a "stickier" ecosystem that is harder to leave.
